历史上发生了些什么？

No Child Left Behind Act

  Requires all public schools receiving federal funding to support a statewide standardized test to all students every year. So students are required to do a annually test, just as the title, no child left behind. Schools that miss AYP for a second consecutive year are being said as in need of improvement, and must develop a two-year improvement plan for the subject that the school is not teaching well. Missing AYP in the third year forces the school to offer free tutoring and other education services to struggling students.

   It created affect on Schools that they have to give tests out, because there are punishments. Some students increased their grades on reading comprehension. The school also make sure that teachers meet the law requirements.

Influenza A virus

  In June 2009, the World Health Organization announced the new strain of swine-origin H1N1 as a pandemic. This strain is often called swine flu. It is a respiratory disease that occurs in pigs which is caused by the Influenza A virus. It is common throughout pig populations worldwide. Broadcast of the virus from pigs to humans is not common.This swine flu outbreak was due to infection with the H1N1 virus and was first observed in Mexico. Many people got infected and hospitalized.

Impact:
   The flue made the government and many medical organizations to take reaction. invest and make useful medications to prevent and cure it. Later Vaccination came out to help prevent and reduce the chances of becoming infected with influenza viruses.

Death of Michael Jackson

  On June 25, 2009, Michael Jackson died of acute propofol and benzodiazepine intoxication at his home. Later, a public memorial service for Jackson was held on July 7 at the Staples Center in Los Angeles, where he had rehearsed for London concerts the night before his death. Jackson’s death triggered an outpouring of reactions around the world, There is Internet traffic, and sales of his music.

Impact:
   Michael Jackson was the most influential artist of the 20th century, who was being called as the king of pop. Musicians in the 20th century had far more cultural impact than any other sort of artist. Michael Jackson had more raw talent as a performer than any of his peers. He was able to package that talent in a whole new way. In both form and content, Jackson simply did what no one had done before.

Columbia Disaster

  On February 1, 2003, the space shuttle Columbia broke apart at Texas, killed all seven crew members on board. The disaster occurred minutes before It was scheduled to land at Florida’s Kennedy Space Center. It was later determined that shortly after launch on January 16, a piece of foam insulation broke off and damaged the shuttle’s left wing. The Columbia disaster was the second major tragedy in the history of the space shuttle program. This space shuttle was originally scheduled to launch on January 11, 2001, but was delayed numerous times for a variety of reasons over nearly two years. The aftermath of the Columbia disaster, the space shuttle program was grounded until July 26, 2005, but In July 2011, the space shuttle program completed its final mission, flown by Atlantis.

Impact:
  Ten years after the devastating Columbia space shuttle accident that took the lives of seven crew members, when NASA will build a new spacecraft that take humans farther into space, they will incorporate the safety lessons learned from the disaster. All of the lessons the agency learned were incorporated into every subsequent flight NASA flew, and are now being used to inform the design of its next-generation spaceship, Orion.

The Race on the White House

  There is an president race in 2000, It was being said it is “The wildest election in history” by the Time magazine. The Democrats chose Vice President Al Gore to take over Bill Clinton. But on the other hand, Republicans supported George W. Bush. On the day before the election night, the data showed that their number of votes are very close, it might take up to a month to determine who is the winner. As election night begin, Al Gore appeared to have more vote, the television then started to project that Gore would win in Florida, but later they took back their words on Gore winning, claimed it was “too close to call.” weeks following the election, a big chaos of the misread on ballots occurred. The public learned that there are voting irregularities in many counties in Florida, where George’s brother was the governor. Gore had requested recounted in four most democratic counties, it started on November 12, but republicans sued to stop the recounts, therefore, a month long court case started. the battle had reached to the supreme court. On the same day, the court voted 5 to 4 to stop the recounts, they claimed that manual recounts lacked uniform standards, it violated the equal protection for voters. The next day, Gore had conceded and Bush accepted the victory.

Impact:
   The election of 2000 have a deep and lasting effect on the the elections that follows, in election law reforms. Some reforms focus on ballots and voting machines; it is clear that the old machines and paper ballots are too old and not accurate. A uniform ballot seems likely to be in place before too long. Other reforms will produce more descriptor legal processes for recounts, so that we are not dealing with the same confusion that caused so much trouble in 2000.

The first black president

  On November 4, 2008, Senator of Illinois, Barack Obama was elected for the president of the United States John McCain. Obama became the 44th president yet also the first African American president. He was elected again to a second term over Mitt Romney, a Massachusetts governor. In 1996, Obama officially first got his political career, winning election to the Illinois State Senate as a Democrat. In 2004, the state delivered 70 percent of its votes to Obama as only the third African American elected to the U.S. Senate since Reconstruction. A crushing financial crisis in the months made the election shifted focus to economic issues, Obama and McCain worked to show they had the best plan for economic. Later, Obama had won the vote and became the first African American President.

Impact:
  President Obama had passed the Health Care Reform, signed the Affordable Care Act, It will cover 32 million uninsured Americans beginning in 2014, good results are true pool people can get a better life, and life securing, but some people even they have money they still use the program which made people mad, because the money is from their tax. He also ended the War in Iraq, less death of American lives,he ordered all U.S. military forces out of the country. Obama’s election also has inspired more black candidates to run for presidential or other election.

Same sex marriage

  On June 26, 2015, the Court concurred that gay marriage is a constitutional right, so all 50 states must allow it and that all other existing bans are invalid. There is a case, Obergefell v. Hodges, is a combine of six lawsuits from Kentucky, Michigan, Ohio and Tennessee. Jim Obergefell and John Arthur, sued Ohio in 2013. John Arthur was dying because of ALS. The couple wanted the state to acknowledge their marriage on Arthur’s death certificate. Obergefell and Arthur had married in Maryland, but their marriage was not recognized. Arthur passed away, and a federal judge ruled that Ohio must recognize the legal union. The state’s appeal was successful. At last,The Court came with a result that marriage is a constitutional right protected by the 14th Amendment and that states cannot deny it, including same-sex couples.

Impact:
   The legalization of same-sex marriage benefits both LGBT people and America as a nation. It brought Equality and Non-Discrimination in Society, everyone should be equal, they also deserve the right to be married. It also provided economically, business now have new opportunities. According to the basic rights, it brought true freedom, equal rights.

Boston Marathon Bombing

  On April 15, 2013, two bombs went off near the finish line of the Boston Marathon, killing three spectators and wounding more than 260 other people. It occurred on April 15, 2013, the 117th running of the Boston Marathon, the world’s oldest annual marathon. At that afternoon, when runners are still in the race, two pressure-cooker bombs was hidden in backpacks that were placed on the ground marathon, It exploded within seconds near the finish line. Investigators later found out that the Tsarnaev’s, who spent part of their childhoods in the former Soviet republic of Kyrgyzstan but lived in the United States, planned to carried out the attack on their own. Dzhokhar Tsarnaev is scheduled to stand trial in November 2014, federal prosecutors announced they will seek the death penalty.

Impact:
  The bombing dragged the tension again, it impacted on people’s daily life, some of them suffered from both physical and mental pain. It also made governments and organizations to search more on what had happened, and their relationships to USSR. After this event happened, safety issues are highlighted when there is a public events. more security on people that are participating.

Cuban Thaw

  The Cuban Thaw is a warming between the United States and Cuba that began 2014, ending the 54-year stretch of antagonism between the two countries. In March 2016, Barack Obama became the first U.S. President to visit Cuba since 1928. On December 17, 2014, U.S. President Barack Obama and Cuban President Raúl Castro announced the beginning of a process of normalizing relations between Cuba and the United States. They had talked about it for months. The agreement would appear on some restrictions, fewer restrictions on remittances, U.S. banks’ access to the Cuban financial system.

Impact:
   On April 14, 2015, the Obama administration announced that Cuba would be removed from the United States State Sponsors of Terrorism list. In 2012, Cuban prisoners the U.S. had convicted of espionage known as the Cuban Five, in prison in the U.S. since the 1990s, would be returned to Cuba in exchange for USAID contractor Alan Gross. He had been imprisoned in Cuba for providing illegal cellphone chips of a type used by CIA agents. This also made it easier for citizens to travel to Cuba. Because of this event, people are controversial about President Obama’s doing, some think it is wrong, others don’t.

Afghanistan War

  There is another war besides the Iraq war, which is the Afghanistan war. It was caused by the September 11 attacks, three phases are concluded. Begin in 2001, first is to overthrow the Taliban, the ultraconservative faction that ruled Afghanistan and provided sanctuary for al-Qaeda. Then secondly, from 2002-2008, was to use U.S. strategy defeating the Taliban militarily and rebuild institutions of the Afghan state. Thirdly, President Obama made a decision in 2009 to temporarily increase the U.S. troop presence in Afghanistan, to protecting the population from Taliban attacks and supporting efforts to rehabilitate insurgents into Afghan society. the U.S. formally ended 2014, the war is the longest that the United States ever fought.

Impact:
   War always bring bad and good affects on people. The American troops often suffer with STPD. There were destabilization nearby Pakistan, which has 100-200 nuclear weapons and an insurgency trying to overthrow the Pakistani government. made large pressure on people that are participating. The country also lost a lot of money on joining the war. However, it is on nation’s safety, after the September 11 attack. Citizens are very concerned, so the government need to insure it.

Osama bin Laden killed by U.S. forces

  In 2011, announced by President Obama, Osama bin Laden, the head of the terrorist group behind the 911 attacks in the United States, is killed by U.S. forces in Pakistan. The raid began around 1 a.m. when U.S. Navy SEALs in two Black Hawk helicopters descended on the compound in a military center of Pakistan’s capital, Islamabad. One of the helicopters crash-landed into the compound, During the raid, five people, including bin Laden and one of his adult sons, were killed by U.S. gunfire. No Americans were injured. Based on evidences SEALs collected during the raid, it was known that bin Laden was planning on assassinate President Obama and other attacks against the U.S. , including one on the anniversary of September 11. The death of Osama bin Laden successfully prevented series of tragic from happening again.

Impact:
   No man has had a greater impact on American culture in the 21st century than Osama bin Laden. The deaths he made 10 years ago led the nation to spend more than a trillion dollars, to help a homeland security. He caused a lot of innocent deaths, government spend a lot of money on rebuilding a safe land and to capture him, preventing any tragedy from happening. There are many changing occurred. like urban design and architecture, requiring bollards and barricades. Airports changed forever, they required more security check.

Financial Crisis

  In 2008 the world economy faced its most dangerous crisis since the Great Depression. It started in 2007 when high home prices in the United States finally turned downward, It first spread to the entire U.S. and then to financial markets overseas. The affect in the U.S. include the entire investment banking industry, insurance company, savings and loan and commercial banks are shutting down. This financial crisis did not only affect the United States but other countries too, like Japan, China, UK. By the end of the year, all of the world’s major economies were in recession. In the final four months of 2008, the U.S. lost nearly two million jobs.

Impact:
  Over the short term, the financial crisis affected the banking sector by causing banks to lose money on mortgage defaults, interbank lending to freeze, and credit to consumers and businesses to dry up.When increasing numbers of U.S. consumers defaulted on their mortgage loans, U.S. banks lost money on the loans, and so did banks in other countries. Banks stopped lending to each other, and it became tougher for consumers and businesses to get credit. It become hard for the business to keep going, because they couldn’t get money from the banks, and banks loans are also stopped because they are losing money and other money isn’t coming in.

Sandy Hook Elementary School shooting

  A shooting had occurred in Connecticut, on Friday morning which had left 28 people dead, 20 of them are children. They are killed inside of their elementary school which is named Sandy Hook Elementary. The man who shoot, is Adam Lanza, he first killed his mother, Nancy Lanza, at the home. Then he drove to Sandy Hook Elementary, where her mom teach. But when the police found the gun shooter, he already killed himself.

Impact:
   The shooting became the second-deadliest in U.S. history, and President Obama, in one of his most emotional speeches as president, wiped away tears as he spoke about the shooting from the White House’s briefing room about this event. This made the government and citizens to bring more attention to school security and the safety of children. More mandatory lockdown, fire drills. In order to let the student know what to do when in a situation.

Iraq War

  In 2003, the United States invaded Iraq, because of two reasons. First, Iraq had mass destructive weapons that they refuse to give up, secondly, there was or may have been a link between Saddam Hussein’s Iraq and the attack of 9-11. so the United States, United Kingdom along with coalition of other countries invaded Iraq. Then Saddam Hussein was ousted, Iraq formed a new government that is more democratic. However, the war still went on. By the end of 2006, more than 3000 American soldiers had been killed and another 20,000 wounded.

Impact:
   There are many affects of the war. When the war started, the administration predicted it would cost around $50-60 billion in total. According to a recent study, the war is set to have cost the U.S $2.2 trillion. It costed way too much money. There are also a lot of pressure on American soldiers, They are charged with fighting the war were stretched to their limits,over 4,000 U.S. troops died during the country’s time in Iraq, Later, the cost of providing medical care to veterans has doubled. Up to 35 percent of Iraq War veterans will suffer from PTSD the suicide rate among veterans has jumped to22 per day. Soldiers fought for the country while risking their lives and other health issues, but from the result of the war, the U.S. is worse off because of the war.

September 11 Attack

  On September 11, 2001,19 Arab terrorists hijacked four airliners and carried out suicide attacks to target the United States. The first plane crashed into the World Trade center and exploded, about 20 mins later, a second plane crashed into the south tower. the chaos is still not over, less than an hour later, the third hijacked plane flew into the southwest side of pentagon in Virginia. As these are happening, passengers on the fourth hijacked plane used their cell phones and acknowledged the other plane crashes. Some of the passengers rushed to prevent the hijackers from striking the White house or the capitol, at the end, plane crashed into an empty field in Pennsylvania. This is the largest terrorist attack ever on U.S. soil, which left nearly 3,000 people dead.

Impact:
   The September 11 attack brought America’s attention in revolving into war of Terrorism. Change concerns about people’s safety and vigilante. The government created new generation of policies like the USA Patriot Act. It give priority to national security. These changes also had effects across the globe, For example, in the Middle East, American military have helped foment rebellions and unrest throughout the region. The September 11 attack survivors faced mental health issues for many years.
